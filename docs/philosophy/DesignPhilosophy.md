# Design Philosophy

Your Fantasy is built around one core belief:

> A player's time should feel meaningful.

The game is not designed around a chosen hero, a mandatory main storyline, or a single correct way to play. Instead, every player should be able to create their own fantasy through exploration, professions, combat, crafting, commerce, and community.

## Core Principles

### Respect the Player's Time

Every player who spends time in Your Fantasy should feel that their time was worthwhile.

Progress should not only be measured by combat power, gold earned, or efficiency. A player who spends an evening fishing, tailoring, decorating a home, helping a friend, exploring a forest, or running a shop should still feel like they meaningfully participated in the world.

### No Chosen Hero

There is no single main character.

The world does not revolve around one scripted hero storyline. Instead, the world is shaped by the players who live in it.

### No One Player Can Do Everything

Specialization matters.

A master blacksmith should need gatherers. A powerful mage should need tailors. Adventurers should rely on crafters, merchants, healers, and support players.

This creates social interdependence and gives every profession a meaningful place in the world.

### Every Profession Should Feel Complete

Combat is only one way to play.

A player should be able to dedicate themselves to becoming a seamstress, blacksmith, chef, fisherman, merchant, explorer, healer, or guild leader and still feel like they are playing a complete game.

### Cooperation Over Individual Power

Your Fantasy should reward communities, friendships, guilds, and player relationships.

The strongest players are not only those with the highest stats, but those who contribute meaningfully to the world around them.

### Meaningful Consequences

Choices should matter.

Death, progression, specialization, crafting, exploration, and reputation should all carry weight. The goal is not to punish players unfairly, but to make the world feel meaningful and alive.

### Player Expression

Players should be able to express who they are through their profession, clothing, housing, reputation, social relationships, and playstyle.

A master artisan should feel just as recognizable and respected as a legendary warrior.

## Long-Term Vision

Your Fantasy aims to become a persistent multiplayer world where players create stories worth remembering.

The goal is not to force every player into the same path, but to create a world where each player can say:

> This was my fantasy.
